Carmen Russoniello, PhD, LRT, LPC, BCB, BCN has more than 20 years of clinical experience as a therapist/counselor, as well as a researcher on the effects of gaming and biofeedback on stress-related medical disorders. He is currently Professor and Director of the Center for Applied Psychophysiology at East Carolina University and serves as scientific advisor to Biocom Technologies. Dr Russoniello is a fellow in both general and neurofeedback from the Biofeedback Certification International Alliance (BCIA) and is a past member of this board, where he served as chair of the ethics committee. Dr Russoniello is also past president of the Association for Applied Psychophysiology and Biofeedback and is past president of the American Therapeutic Recreation Association.

Dr. Russoniello teaches undergraduate and graduate biofeedback courses through a first-of-its-kind global classroom initiative and directs a biofeedback program for Wounded Warrior Marines at Camp Lejeune. The novel biofeedback intervention involves EEG and heart rate variability (HRV) feedback and includes the use of virtual reality. Dr. Russoniello is himself a former Marine machine gunner and decorated Vietnam combat veteran. Personal interests include running (13 marathons, including Boston x2), dogs, fishing, and gardening.

Dr. Russoniello's psychophysiological research on video games, mood, and stress has been featured in the New York Times, Washington Post, Newsweek, Wired Magazine, CNN, BBC, Ladies Home Journal, Women's World, Web MD and numerous other publications. His previous research on the physiological and biochemical aspects of recreation participation has been included in the President's Council on Physical Activity and his research on the prevalence and treatment of post traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) in children after natural disasters has been cited in a number of government publications including the Department of Ecology and the Veterans Administration's Center for Post Traumatic Stress Disorder.

Matthew Fish, PhD., LRT/CTRS, LPCA, BCB, Assistant Professor, has joined the faculty of East Carolina University in the Department of Recreation and Leisure Studies. His duties include teaching Stress Management, Introduction to Leisure Societies, Advanced Biofeedback Lab, and serve as a RCLS Undergraduate Society faculty advisor.

Dr. Fish received his BS and MS in Recreational Therapy and Recreational Therapy Administration from East Carolina University, respectively. Dr. Fish received his PhD in Rehabilitation Counseling and Administration at East Carolina University. Prior to joining ECU, Dr. Fish worked as a Recreational Therapist in the Greenville community and has more than 9 years of experience with biofeedback and psychophysiology.

Dr. Fish's research interests include psychophysiology of recreational activities, stress management, the prescriptive use of casual video games for decreasing mental health symptoms, mobile technology, as well as, addictions and substance abuse. In his down time, Dr. Fish enjoys spending time with his wife, Julia, two dogs, Kayla May and Biscuit, lifting weights, darts, and outdoor activities.
